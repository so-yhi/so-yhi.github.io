  • To keep people engaged, you have to keep them beliving in the world you create
  • Level should not be too frustrating or boring
  • You have to manage the pacing of the level and properly challenge the player
  • Difficulty should go up gradually: either by increasing the complexity or by adding more ennemies.
  • Respect the technicals constraint of the plateform (e.g.: on a console you have a cnotroller, on computer, a keyboard and mouse) (ndlr: you may not enjoy playing WoW with a controller…).
  • On mobile phone, player tend to play for shorter periods of time than on console or pc: adapt the length of your level
  • Consider the audiance (focus on targeted audiance): demographics, interest etc… It will affect the tone and the palette of the game
  • Use of storytelling, for example to introduce a new weapon, specific mechanic or ability
  • Find how the player moves around the map, the interraction with PNG, enemies or objects. Is the figthing style long-distance or closer.
  • List all the level parameter
  • Map the level, have an overall vision. Divide the level in smaller sections
  • Pay attention to the kind of place the games happens: jungle? Old city?
  • Make a draft with the size and relative size of decor and object. Label the plateform, the obstruction point and enemies (and their trigger point)
  • Finally, test the design
